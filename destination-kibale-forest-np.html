Kibale Forest NP

⏱ 3 Nights Stop 🌤 Humid rainforest, ~25°C (77°F). 🎟 Permit Required ($300)

Destination Overview

Kibale Forest National Park is celebrated as the primate capital of East Africa. This dense tropical rainforest shelters 13 primate species, most notably a population of over 1,500 wild chimpanzees. Guided chimpanzee tracking here offers an unparalleled opportunity to observe these highly intelligent human relatives grooming, feeding, and swinging through the canopy. The park also features the Bigodi Wetland Sanctuary, a paradise for birdwatchers and primate walks.

Possible Activities

✔
Chimpanzee Tracking to encounter chimpanzees in their natural habitat.
✔
Guided walk through Bigodi Wetland Sanctuary for birding and primates.
✔
Chimpanzee Habituation Experience (CHEX) to spend a full day with researchers and chimpanzees.
✔
Nocturnal forest walk to spot bushbabies, pottos, and nightjars.
✔
Explore the Crater Lakes region and hike to the 'Top of the World' viewpoint.
✔
Visit the Amabere ga Nyinamwiru caves and waterfalls in Fort Portal.
